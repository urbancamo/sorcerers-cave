Sorcerer's Cave — Fight UI Mockup of the player-driven, drag-to-pair combat surface using the real creature cards. Static — nothing is interactive.

Party fighter Caster (Priest / Wizard) Stranger (foe) Wielded artefact (tucked on the card corner)

1 · Setting up the round — pairing off

The strangers are laid out in a line as their real cards. You drag each party card onto a foe to form a match; drop a second fighter to send two-against-one; drop a Priest/Wizard onto a match's ✦ background slot to lend magical power. Wielded artefacts (e.g. the Hero's Magic Sword) ride on the card's corner and add to its strength. Live totals update as you build; “Roll the round” stays disabled until the plan is legal.

2 · Out-numbered — engine forms the strangers' strongest combination

When you can't engage every foe, you assign who you can; the engine auto-forms the strangers' strongest combination against your fighter (§395) and shows it. Foes you can't reach this round are dimmed “unengaged”.

3 · Mid-drag — valid vs invalid drop targets; a wielded Magic Staff

4 · Resolving — a die per match

On “Roll”, each match resolves with the existing dice overlay: both d6 plus each side's total (strength + roll + surprise/ring − curses). Winner slays the loser; a tie spares both.

5 · Casualty choice — when a two-fighter match is lost

If a pair loses (and both are mortal), you pick who falls; a d6 then decides — 4–6 grants your preference. Background casters are never vulnerable.

6 · Between turns & retreat

A round ends your turn (multiplayer): other parties act, then your fight resumes — you re-pair freely from scratch. You may retreat by any doorway/stair after one round; a dead-end forces another round this turn, and any treasure dropped in the area is left behind.

Accessibility / mobile: every drag has a tap equivalent — tap a fighter to pick it up, tap a foe (or a match's ✦) to place it; tap again to send two, or tap the tray to free it. Fully usable without a pointer (the runner-up “tap-to-assign” UI, kept as the keyboard/touch path).

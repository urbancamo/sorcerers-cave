Design Plan & Interactive Mockups

The Sorcerer's Cave A Mobile-Phone Experience

Keeping the signature 3D cave and floating-panel HUD — but making both behave on a phone, in portrait and landscape, with nothing overlapping and every doorway reachable by thumb.

2026 · 06 · 29Scope · Plan + MockupTheme · Authentic in-game tokens
I

What breaks on a phone today

Three concrete faults, drawn straight from the current build.

⛌

Overlapping panels

Every HUD region is pinned with hard pixel offsets — top:84px, left:26px, top:112px. On a ~375px screen the brand, stats, roster and prompt collide in the centre. Nothing reflows.

⮂

Invisible doorway arrows

Exits are 3D cones rotated in world space. In portrait the camera squeezes the horizontal axis, so the East/West chevrons turn edge-on and slide off-frame — you can't see where you may go.

⤡

No orientation sense

The layout assumes a wide landscape window (h-screen w-screen). Portrait clips the bottom panels; rotating the device triggers no reflow. There is no mobile-first path at all.

II

The approach — fix what's there

Lightest touch that solves all three, true to the game's look.

◬

Billboarded chevrons

Doorway markers always face the camera and stay legible — never edge-on. Their world position at the doorway still carries the direction, with a bigger invisible tap target for thumbs.

⊟

Auto-framed camera

The camera fits the active chamber and all its exits to whatever viewport it's in, per orientation — so the full cross of arrows always sits comfortably inside the frame.

❖

Fluid floating panels

The translucent panels stay — but become width/orientation-driven, not pixel-pinned. They shrink, gain transparency, collapse to handles, and reflow so they can never overlap each other or the map.

IV

Why billboarding is safe

Direction is a property of the doorway — not of the screen.

N
S
E
W
Camera orbited 18° · arrows still upright & correct

Today each marker already stores its semantic direction and the tap reads that, not a screen position. So billboarding — rotating the glyph to face the camera — only changes how it looks, never where it sits or what it does.

cave3d.js:240  grp.userData.move = m.dir — marker carries N/E/S/W/U/D
cave3d.js:245  positioned by absolute world direction (art rotation is cosmetic)
cave3d.js:753  a tap raycasts the marker → reads its stored direction
cave3d.js:467 → 477  doMove(dir) → engine.tryMove(dir)

Because the marker is anchored to the doorway in world space, orbiting the camera or rotating a tile's art can't desync it — the right-edge arrow is always the East exit and always moves you East.

This is exactly why we did not use a screen-space D-pad: a D-pad's "up" drifts as the camera orbits and would point the wrong way. Position carries the meaning; billboarding only carries the visibility.
